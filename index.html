The Jangma Clause — July Is Monsoon Season

Expect 29–33°C, 80% humidity, and sudden torrential bursts between steamy clear gaps — not all-day drizzle. Carry a compact umbrella always; wear quick-dry, not cotton. The plan already front-loads outdoor stops before 11:00 and after 17:30, and every day has an air-conditioned anchor.

If a day washes out, swap in: The Hyundai Seoul · National Museum of Korea (free, open every day of your trip) · Starfield COEX · Mangwon Market (covered arcade) · Seoul Sky (check the view webcam first) · any department-store food hall. Cheonggyecheon floods and closes in heavy rain — sirens mean leave the channel. Banpo fountain and river cruises cancel in rain/wind; the fountain is your Day 1 night show, so slide it to any clear evening if Day 1 washes out.

What you can't get in July: Gyeongbokgung night opening (spring session ended Jun 14, resumes Sep) and the Hangang Summer Festival (starts Jul 26). The Banpo Moonlight Rainbow Fountain runs nightly all July — that's your free night show, set for Day 1.

Not Going to Daejeon? Your Seoul Monday

For anyone sitting out the esports — Monday is the trickiest day to freelance, since most palaces and museums that close Mondays are shut (Changdeokgung, Deoksugung, Jongmyo, Leeum, Oil Tank Park). Here's a Monday-proof, monsoon-proof menu that needs zero coordination with the Daejeon crew, who roll back into Hongik around 00:15 — plan your own dinner and don't wait up.

  • ESPORTS · SEOULLoL Park, Jongno — the LCK floor is dark while MSI runs, so the Riot Store, Cafe Bilgewater and the 24-h PC bang are quiet and yours. Grab a PC and watch the Daejeon broadcast together, or catch it at a Hongdae bar — you still get the match.
  • RAINPROOFThe Hyundai Seoul + Starfield COEX — Seoul's two cathedral-malls: Starfield Library, the COEX Aquarium, the Hyundai's indoor garden. The honest answer to a 33°C downpour, both open Monday.
  • RIVERHangang park & the outdoor pools — Mangwon park is a walk from base; the riverside pools at Ttukseom or Yeouido (Jun–Aug, ~₩5k) are peak Seoul summer. Watch the sky and go in a clear gap.
  • NEAR BASEHongdae / Yeonnam home turf — don't overreach on a Monday: the Gyeongui Line Forest, Yeonnam cafes and thrift, and Mangwon Market's covered arcade for dinner are all a walk from the door.
  • RESETJjimjilbang (Dragon Hill Spa, Yongsan) — a 24-h Korean bathhouse to soak, sauna and nap through the worst of the heat. The lowest-effort monsoon Monday there is.

Want more? The Extras menu at the foot of the plan is full of Monday-safe, non-history fillers too — Namsan at the 19:55 sunset, Myeongdong shopping, Noryangjin fish market, the Mullae art village.

No ticket, or T1 isn't drawn for a Monday slot? Don't spend the day on trains. This whole slot then becomes a Gyeongbokgung Monday — it's the one major palace open Mondays (when Changdeokgung and the others are shut): the throne hall, Seochon and Daeo Bookstore, samgyetang at Tosokchon, and Gwanghwamun + Cheonggyecheon at dusk. You'd then lighten Wednesday's west-palace day to suit. Either way, Monday is covered.
